VERMICOMPOST PRODUCTION GUIDE
==============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Vermicompost is the product of composting using earthworms to create a nutrient-rich organic fertilizer. It is produced by Eisenia fetida (red wiggler) and Lumbricus rubellus. Vermicompost improves soil structure, water retention, and microbial activity.

2. EARTHWORM SPECIES
- Eisenia fetida (Red wiggler): Most commonly used; tolerates wide temperature range
- Lumbricus rubellus: Good for composting
- Perionyx excavatus: Suitable for tropical conditions (India)
- Eudrilus eugeniae: African nightcrawler; fast decomposer

3. NUTRIENT CONTENT OF VERMICOMPOST
- Nitrogen: 2.5–3.0%
- Phosphorus: 1.5–2.0%
- Potassium: 1.5–2.0%
- Calcium: 4–5%
- Magnesium: 0.5–1.0%
- Micronutrients: Fe, Zn, Mn, Cu in plant-available forms

4. VERMICOMPOSTING METHODS

4.1 Bed Method
- Prepare beds 1 m wide, 0.5 m high, any length
- Fill with partially decomposed organic matter
- Introduce earthworms at 1 kg/m²
- Maintain moisture at 60–70%
- Cover with gunny bags or straw

4.2 Pit Method
- Dig pits 2×1×0.75 m
- Line with bricks to prevent escape
- Fill with organic matter in layers
- Introduce earthworms

4.3 Windrow Method
- Long rows of organic matter
- Suitable for large-scale production

5. RAW MATERIALS
- Crop residues: Straw, stalks, leaves
- Animal dung: Cow, buffalo, goat (partially decomposed)
- Kitchen waste: Vegetable peels, fruit waste
- Green waste: Grass clippings, weeds
- Avoid: Meat, dairy, oily materials, diseased plant material

6. PRODUCTION PROCESS
- Layer organic matter 15–20 cm thick
- Add partially decomposed cow dung as inoculant
- Introduce earthworms (1 kg/m²)
- Maintain moisture 60–70% (squeeze test: few drops)
- Temperature: 20–30°C
- Avoid direct sunlight
- Turn material every 15 days
- Harvest in 45–60 days

7. HARVESTING
- Stop watering 7 days before harvest
- Earthworms migrate to bottom
- Collect vermicompost from top
- Sieve through 4 mm mesh
- Dry to 30–40% moisture

8. APPLICATION RATES
- Field crops: 2–5 t/ha
- Vegetables: 5–10 t/ha
- Fruit crops: 5–10 kg/plant
- Nursery: Mix 20–30% with potting mix

9. BENEFITS
- Improves soil structure and aeration
- Increases water holding capacity
- Enhances microbial activity
- Provides slow-release nutrients
- Suppresses soil-borne diseases
- Improves seed germination

10. VERMICOMPOST TEA
- Dilute vermicompost 1:10 with water
- Aerate for 24 hours
- Apply as foliar spray or soil drench
- Improves plant growth and disease resistance
