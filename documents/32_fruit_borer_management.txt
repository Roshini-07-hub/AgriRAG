FRUIT BORER MANAGEMENT
======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Fruit borers are major pests of vegetables and fruits. Key species: Leucinodes orbonalis (brinjal fruit and shoot borer), Helicoverpa armigera (tomato fruit borer), Conogethes punctiferalis (guava fruit borer).

2. BRINJAL FRUIT AND SHOOT BORER (Leucinodes orbonalis)

2.1 Identification
- Adult: White moth with pink and brown markings
- Larvae: Pink with brown head, 18–20 mm long

2.2 Damage
- Shoot borer: Wilting of young shoots (dead heart)
- Fruit borer: Entry hole with frass; internal feeding
- Infested fruits rot and drop prematurely

2.3 Economic Threshold
- 5% shoot infestation or 5% fruit infestation

3. TOMATO FRUIT BORER (Helicoverpa armigera)

3.1 Identification
- Adult: Stout moth, yellowish-brown
- Larvae: Variable color, 35–40 mm

3.2 Damage
- Circular entry hole on fruit
- Internal feeding on seeds and pulp
- Frass at entry hole
- Fruit rotting

4. GUAVA FRUIT BORER (Conogethes punctiferalis)

4.1 Damage
- Larvae bore into developing fruits
- Frass at entry hole
- Premature fruit drop

5. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

5.1 Cultural Control
- Remove and destroy infested shoots and fruits
- Deep summer ploughing to expose pupae
- Crop rotation
- Avoid ratoon crops

5.2 Biological Control
- Trichogramma chilonis: Release at egg laying stage
- Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki: 1 kg/ha
- NPV spray for Helicoverpa

5.3 Chemical Control
For brinjal fruit and shoot borer:
- Spinosad 45 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Emamectin benzoate 5 SG at 0.4 g/L
- Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Indoxacarb 14.5 SC at 1 mL/L

For tomato fruit borer:
- Emamectin benzoate 5 SG at 0.4 g/L
- Spinosad 45 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Flubendiamide 20 WG at 0.5 g/L

5.4 Pheromone Traps
- Install 5 traps per hectare for monitoring
- Mass trapping with delta traps

6. SPRAY SCHEDULE
- Begin spraying at first flower appearance
- Spray at 7–10 day intervals
- Rotate insecticides to prevent resistance
