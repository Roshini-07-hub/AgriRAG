DROUGHT MANAGEMENT IN AGRICULTURE
===================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall leading to water shortage. It is the most common and widespread natural disaster affecting agriculture. India experiences drought in 16–18% of its geographical area annually.

2. TYPES OF DROUGHT
- Meteorological drought: Below-normal rainfall
- Hydrological drought: Reduced streamflow and groundwater
- Agricultural drought: Soil moisture deficit affecting crops
- Socioeconomic drought: Water shortage affecting economy

3. DROUGHT-PRONE AREAS IN INDIA
- Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra (Marathwada, Vidarbha)
- Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu
- Odisha, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh

4. CROP MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

4.1 Drought-Tolerant Varieties
- Rice: Sahbhagi Dhan, DRR Dhan-44, Swarna Sub-1
- Wheat: HD-2781, K-9107
- Maize: HQPM-1, DHM-117
- Sorghum: CSH-16, SPV-462
- Pearl millet: HHB-67, GHB-558

4.2 Contingency Crop Planning
- Substitute long-duration crops with short-duration varieties
- Switch to drought-tolerant crops (millets, pulses)
- Sow short-duration varieties if monsoon is delayed
- Contingency plans prepared by ICAR for each district

4.3 Crop Diversification
- Shift from water-intensive crops (rice, sugarcane) to millets, pulses
- Intercropping to spread risk
- Horticulture crops with drip irrigation

5. SOIL MOISTURE CONSERVATION
- Mulching: Reduces evaporation by 30–50%
- Minimum tillage: Preserves soil moisture
- Tied ridges and furrows
- Contour bunding

6. WATER MANAGEMENT
- Prioritize irrigation for critical crop stages
- Deficit irrigation: Apply 60–70% of full requirement
- Alternate furrow irrigation
- Rainwater harvesting for supplemental irrigation

7. STRESS MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUES
- Anti-transpirants: Kaolin 5%, Waxol spray
- Potassium application improves drought tolerance
- Zinc application improves stress tolerance
- Foliar spray of KH2PO4 0.5% at stress

8. LIVESTOCK MANAGEMENT DURING DROUGHT
- Provide supplementary feed
- Ensure water availability
- Destocking if fodder is unavailable
- Fodder banks and silage preparation

9. GOVERNMENT RELIEF MEASURES
- National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF)
- Crop insurance under PMFBY
- Input subsidy for affected farmers
- Employment guarantee (MGNREGS)
- Drinking water supply

10. EARLY WARNING SYSTEMS
- India Meteorological Department (IMD) forecasts
- Drought Early Warning System (DEWS)
- Satellite-based vegetation monitoring (NDVI)
- Crop condition assessment
