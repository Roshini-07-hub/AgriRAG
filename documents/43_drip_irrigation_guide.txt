DRIP IRRIGATION GUIDE
=====================

1. INTRODUCTION
Drip irrigation (trickle irrigation) is a micro-irrigation system that delivers water directly to the root zone of plants through a network of pipes, tubes, and emitters. It is the most efficient irrigation method, saving 30–50% water compared to flood irrigation.

2. COMPONENTS OF DRIP SYSTEM
- Water source: Well, canal, tank, river
- Pump and motor: To pressurize water
- Filter unit: Sand filter + screen filter + disc filter
- Fertilizer injector (Venturi or fertigation tank)
- Main line: PVC pipe (63–110 mm diameter)
- Sub-main line: PVC pipe (40–63 mm)
- Lateral line: LLDPE pipe (12–16 mm)
- Emitters/drippers: 2–8 L/hour discharge

3. TYPES OF EMITTERS
- Inline drippers: Embedded in lateral pipe
- Online drippers: Inserted into lateral pipe
- Micro-sprinklers: For wider coverage
- Foggers: For humidity-sensitive crops

4. DESIGN PARAMETERS
- Operating pressure: 1.0–1.5 kg/cm²
- Emission uniformity: >90%
- Dripper spacing: 30–60 cm (row crops), 60–90 cm (orchards)
- Lateral spacing: Equal to row spacing

5. CROP-WISE WATER REQUIREMENTS (L/plant/day)
- Banana: 8–12 L
- Sugarcane: 3–5 L per meter row
- Tomato: 1.5–2.5 L
- Cotton: 2–3 L
- Onion: 0.5–1.0 L
- Grapes: 4–6 L

6. FERTIGATION THROUGH DRIP
- Apply water-soluble fertilizers through drip system
- Improves fertilizer use efficiency by 30–40%
- Recommended fertilizers: Urea, MAP, MKP, KNO3
- Apply in 3 equal splits: 1/3 at start, 1/3 in middle, 1/3 at end of irrigation

7. INSTALLATION PROCEDURE
- Survey and design the system
- Lay main and sub-main lines
- Install filter unit and fertigation equipment
- Lay lateral lines along crop rows
- Install drippers at plant positions
- Flush system before use

8. MAINTENANCE
- Flush laterals weekly
- Clean filters daily during operation
- Check for clogged drippers monthly
- Acid treatment (HCl 0.1%) for calcium deposits
- Chlorine treatment (5 ppm) for biological clogging

9. GOVERNMENT SUBSIDY
- PM Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)
- Subsidy: 55% for small/marginal farmers, 45% for others
- Apply through state horticulture department

10. ECONOMICS
- Installation cost: Rs. 50,000–1,50,000/ha
- Water saving: 30–50%
- Yield increase: 20–40%
- Payback period: 3–5 years
