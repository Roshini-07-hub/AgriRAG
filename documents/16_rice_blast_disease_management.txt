RICE BLAST DISEASE MANAGEMENT
==============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Rice blast, caused by the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae (formerly Pyricularia oryzae), is the most destructive disease of rice worldwide. It can cause yield losses of 10–30% and up to 100% in severe cases.

2. SYMPTOMS
- Leaf blast: Diamond-shaped lesions with gray center and brown border
- Node blast: Black rotting of nodes causing stem breakage
- Neck blast: Rotting at base of panicle causing "dead head"
- Panicle blast: Partial or complete sterility of grains

3. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 24–28°C
- High humidity (>90%) and prolonged leaf wetness
- Heavy nitrogen application
- Dense planting
- Susceptible varieties

4. DISEASE CYCLE
- Fungus survives in infected straw and seeds
- Conidia spread by wind and rain splash
- Infection occurs through stomata and wounds
- Disease spreads rapidly in cool, humid weather

5. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

5.1 Cultural Control
- Use resistant varieties: Pusa Basmati-1, IR-64, Swarna Sub-1
- Avoid excess nitrogen application
- Maintain proper plant spacing
- Remove infected plant debris
- Avoid late evening irrigation

5.2 Chemical Control
- Seed treatment: Tricyclazole 0.1% solution for 8 hours
- Foliar spray at tillering and panicle initiation:
  * Tricyclazole 75 WP at 0.6 g/L water
  * Isoprothiolane 40 EC at 1.5 mL/L water
  * Carbendazim 50 WP at 1 g/L water
- Repeat spray after 10–15 days if disease persists

5.3 Biological Control
- Pseudomonas fluorescens 0.5% WP at 2.5 kg/ha
- Trichoderma viride seed treatment

6. RESISTANCE MANAGEMENT
- Rotate fungicides with different modes of action
- Avoid repeated use of same fungicide
- Use multi-line varieties

7. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL
- Spray when 2–5% leaf area is affected
- Spray immediately at neck blast appearance
