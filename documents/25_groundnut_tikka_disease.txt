GROUNDNUT TIKKA DISEASE MANAGEMENT
====================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Tikka disease (Cercospora leaf spot) of groundnut is caused by two fungi: Cercospora arachidicola (early leaf spot) and Phaeoisariopsis personata (late leaf spot). It is the most widespread foliar disease of groundnut, causing yield losses of 10–50%.

2. SYMPTOMS

2.1 Early Leaf Spot (Cercospora arachidicola)
- Circular brown spots on upper leaf surface
- Yellow halo around spots
- Spots appear on upper surface first
- Lesions: 1–10 mm diameter

2.2 Late Leaf Spot (Phaeoisariopsis personata)
- Dark brown to black spots on lower leaf surface
- No yellow halo
- Spots appear on lower surface first
- More severe than early leaf spot

3. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 25–31°C
- High humidity (>85%) and rainfall
- Prolonged leaf wetness
- Dense canopy

4. DISEASE CYCLE
- Fungi survive in infected plant debris and soil
- Conidia spread by wind and rain splash
- Infection occurs through stomata
- Disease progresses from lower to upper leaves

5. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

5.1 Cultural Control
- Use disease-free certified seeds
- Crop rotation with cereals
- Remove and destroy infected plant debris
- Avoid dense planting
- Maintain proper field sanitation

5.2 Chemical Control
- Seed treatment: Thiram 3 g/kg + Carbendazim 1 g/kg
- Foliar spray at first symptom appearance (30–35 DAS):
  * Mancozeb 75 WP at 2.5 g/L
  * Chlorothalonil 75 WP at 2 g/L
  * Carbendazim 50 WP at 1 g/L
  * Tebuconazole 25.9 EC at 1 mL/L
- Spray at 10–14 day intervals (3–4 sprays)

5.3 Biological Control
- Trichoderma viride seed treatment
- Pseudomonas fluorescens foliar spray

6. RESISTANT VARIETIES
- ICGV-86031, ICGV-86699 (ICRISAT varieties)
- TAG-24 (tolerant)
- Kadiri-3 (field tolerant)

7. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD
- Spray when 5% leaf area is affected
- Preventive sprays during humid weather

8. INTEGRATED MANAGEMENT
- Combine resistant varieties + crop rotation + timely fungicide application
- Avoid late sowing which increases disease pressure
- Proper drying of pods reduces storage losses
