COTTON LEAF CURL DISEASE MANUAL
================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Cotton Leaf Curl Disease (CLCuD) is caused by Cotton leaf curl virus (CLCuV), a begomovirus transmitted by whitefly (Bemisia tabaci). It is the most devastating disease of cotton in Pakistan and northwestern India, causing yield losses up to 100%.

2. SYMPTOMS
- Upward or downward curling of leaves
- Thickening and darkening of leaf veins
- Enations (leaf-like outgrowths) on underside of leaves
- Stunted plant growth
- Reduced boll formation and fiber quality
- Infected plants remain green but unproductive

3. CAUSAL ORGANISM
- Virus: Cotton leaf curl Multan virus (CLCuMuV) and related begomoviruses
- Vector: Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) — persistent, circulative transmission
- Virus acquired in 15–30 minutes; transmitted in 15–30 minutes
- No seed transmission

4. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- High whitefly population
- Temperature 25–35°C
- Dry weather with low humidity
- Presence of alternate hosts (tomato, okra, tobacco)

5. DISEASE CYCLE
- Virus persists in whitefly and infected plant material
- Whitefly acquires virus from infected plants
- Transmits to healthy plants during feeding
- Disease spreads rapidly in hot dry conditions

6. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

6.1 Resistant Varieties
- Use CLCuD-tolerant varieties: MNH-786, CIM-496
- Bt cotton varieties with CLCuD tolerance
- Avoid susceptible varieties in endemic areas

6.2 Vector (Whitefly) Management
- Seed treatment: Imidacloprid 70 WS at 5 g/kg
- Foliar spray:
  * Imidacloprid 17.8 SL at 0.5 mL/L
  * Thiamethoxam 25 WG at 0.3 g/L
  * Spiromesifen 22.9 SC at 1 mL/L
- Yellow sticky traps: 10–15 per hectare
- Avoid pyrethroid sprays (increase whitefly population)

6.3 Cultural Control
- Early sowing to avoid peak whitefly season
- Remove and destroy infected plants
- Control alternate weed hosts
- Avoid growing susceptible crops near cotton

6.4 Biological Control
- Encarsia formosa (parasitoid of whitefly)
- Chrysoperla carnea (predator)
- Neem-based insecticides: NSKE 5%

7. MONITORING
- Regular scouting for whitefly and disease symptoms
- Economic threshold: 6 whiteflies per leaf
- Spray when 10% plants show symptoms
