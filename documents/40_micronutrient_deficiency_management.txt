MICRONUTRIENT DEFICIENCY MANAGEMENT
=====================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Micronutrients are essential elements required by plants in small quantities. Deficiencies of zinc, iron, boron, manganese, copper, and molybdenum are widespread in Indian soils and cause significant yield losses.

2. ZINC DEFICIENCY

2.1 Symptoms
- Rice: Khaira disease — brown rusty spots on leaves, stunted growth
- Wheat: White striping on leaves
- Maize: White bud — white striping on young leaves
- General: Interveinal chlorosis on young leaves

2.2 Favorable Conditions
- Alkaline soils (pH >7.5)
- Calcareous soils
- Flooded rice soils
- High phosphorus soils

2.3 Management
- Soil application: Zinc sulphate (ZnSO4) 25 kg/ha
- Foliar spray: ZnSO4 0.5% (5 g/L) + lime 0.25%
- Seed treatment: ZnSO4 2% solution

3. IRON DEFICIENCY

3.1 Symptoms
- Interveinal chlorosis on young leaves
- Leaves turn pale yellow to white
- Common in calcareous and alkaline soils

3.2 Management
- Foliar spray: Ferrous sulphate (FeSO4) 0.5% + citric acid 0.1%
- Soil application: FeSO4 50 kg/ha (less effective)
- Chelated iron (Fe-EDTA): 5 kg/ha

4. BORON DEFICIENCY

4.1 Symptoms
- Hollow heart in cauliflower and turnip
- Cracked stem in celery
- Poor fruit set in sunflower and cotton
- Distorted young leaves

4.2 Management
- Soil application: Borax 10–15 kg/ha
- Foliar spray: Borax 0.2% (2 g/L) at flowering

5. MANGANESE DEFICIENCY

5.1 Symptoms
- Interveinal chlorosis on young leaves
- Gray speck in oats
- Marsh spot in peas

5.2 Management
- Foliar spray: MnSO4 0.5% (5 g/L)
- Soil application: MnSO4 25 kg/ha

6. COPPER DEFICIENCY

6.1 Symptoms
- Wilting of young leaves
- Bluish-green discoloration
- Reclamation disease in cereals on peat soils

6.2 Management
- Soil application: CuSO4 25 kg/ha
- Foliar spray: CuSO4 0.2% (2 g/L)

7. MOLYBDENUM DEFICIENCY

7.1 Symptoms
- Whiptail in cauliflower
- Marginal scorch in legumes
- Nitrogen fixation failure in legumes

7.2 Management
- Seed treatment: Ammonium molybdate 1 g/kg seed
- Foliar spray: Ammonium molybdate 0.05%

8. SOIL TEST FOR MICRONUTRIENTS
- DTPA extraction for Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu
- Hot water extraction for B
- Critical levels: Zn <0.6 ppm, Fe <4.5 ppm, Mn <2 ppm, Cu <0.2 ppm, B <0.5 ppm

9. INTEGRATED MICRONUTRIENT MANAGEMENT
- Soil test before application
- Organic matter addition improves micronutrient availability
- Avoid excess phosphorus application
- Maintain soil pH 6.0–7.0 for optimal availability
