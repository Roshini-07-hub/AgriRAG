SUNFLOWER FARMING HANDBOOK
==========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Sunflower (Helianthus annuus) is an important oilseed crop grown in kharif, rabi, and summer seasons. It contains 40–45% oil of high quality with linoleic acid. Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and Maharashtra are major producers.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–30°C; tolerates mild frost at seedling stage
- Rainfall: 500–750 mm
- Requires bright sunshine for oil quality
- Sensitive to waterlogging

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained loamy to clay loam soils
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- Deep soils with good moisture retention
- Avoid saline and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Hybrids: KBSH-44, KBSH-53, MSFH-17
- Open-pollinated: EC-68414, Morden
- High oleic: Hysun-33

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing to 25–30 cm
- 2 harrowings for fine tilth
- Apply 5 t/ha FYM before sowing
- Form ridges and furrows

6. SOWING
- Kharif: June–July; Rabi: October–November; Summer: January–February
- Seed rate: 5–6 kg/ha (hybrid), 8–10 kg/ha (OPV)
- Spacing: 60×30 cm
- Depth: 4–5 cm
- Seed treatment: Thiram 3 g/kg

7. IRRIGATION
- Critical stages: seedling establishment, bud initiation, flowering, seed filling
- Total requirement: 400–600 mm
- Avoid waterlogging at any stage

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 90 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 30 kg/ha (basal)
- Boron: 1.5 kg/ha foliar spray at bud stage

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Inter-cultivation at 20 and 35 DAS
- Avoid herbicides after bud initiation

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest when back of head turns yellow-brown
- Moisture at harvest: 20–25%
- Dry to 9–10% for storage
- Average yield: 1.5–2.5 t/ha

11. USES
- Sunflower oil: Cooking, margarine, cosmetics
- Sunflower meal: Livestock feed
- Seeds: Snack food, bird feed
- Ornamental use in gardens
