WATER CONSERVATION TECHNIQUES IN AGRICULTURE
=============================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Water is the most critical resource for agriculture. With increasing water scarcity, efficient water use is essential for sustainable farming. India uses 80% of its freshwater for agriculture, making conservation critical.

2. IN-SITU WATER CONSERVATION

2.1 Contour Bunding
- Earthen bunds along contour lines
- Reduces runoff and increases infiltration
- Suitable for slopes up to 6%
- Spacing: 30–50 m depending on slope

2.2 Terracing
- Converts slopes into level steps
- Reduces erosion and conserves water
- Suitable for slopes 6–33%
- Types: Bench terraces, broad-based terraces

2.3 Mulching
- Cover soil with organic or plastic mulch
- Reduces evaporation by 30–50%
- Organic mulch: Straw, leaves, crop residues
- Plastic mulch: Black, silver, or transparent

2.4 Tied Ridges
- Small earthen dams across furrows
- Impounds water in furrows
- Increases infiltration
- Suitable for row crops

2.5 Broad Bed and Furrow (BBF)
- Raised beds with furrows for drainage
- Conserves moisture in beds
- Improves drainage in waterlogged areas

3. RAINWATER HARVESTING

3.1 Farm Ponds
- Excavated ponds to collect runoff
- Size: 20×20×3 m for 1 ha catchment
- Lined with plastic or concrete
- Used for supplemental irrigation

3.2 Check Dams
- Small dams across streams
- Recharge groundwater
- Provide water for livestock

3.3 Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting
- Collect rainwater from rooftops
- Store in underground tanks
- Use for domestic and irrigation purposes

4. EFFICIENT IRRIGATION METHODS
- Drip irrigation: 30–50% water saving
- Sprinkler irrigation: 20–30% water saving
- Furrow irrigation: 20% saving over flood
- Alternate furrow irrigation: 30% saving

5. CROP MANAGEMENT FOR WATER CONSERVATION
- Drought-tolerant varieties
- Crop rotation with low water-requiring crops
- Intercropping to maximize water use
- Reduced tillage to conserve soil moisture
- Cover crops to reduce evaporation

6. SOIL MANAGEMENT FOR WATER CONSERVATION
- Increase organic matter to improve water holding capacity
- Deep ploughing to break hardpan
- Subsoiling to improve infiltration
- Avoid soil compaction

7. WATER BUDGETING
- Calculate crop water requirement (ETc = ETo × Kc)
- ETo: Reference evapotranspiration
- Kc: Crop coefficient
- Schedule irrigation based on soil moisture monitoring

8. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES
- PM Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY): Har Khet Ko Pani, More Crop Per Drop
- Watershed Development Programme
- National Water Mission
