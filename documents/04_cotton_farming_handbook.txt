COTTON FARMING HANDBOOK
=======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Cotton (Gossypium hirsutum) is the most important fiber crop and is called "White Gold." India is the largest producer of cotton globally. Major cotton-growing states include Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana, and Punjab.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 21–35°C; frost-free season of 180–200 days
- Rainfall: 500–1000 mm; dry weather at boll opening
- Long sunny days promote fiber quality
- High humidity causes disease problems

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Deep black cotton soils (Vertisols) are ideal
- pH: 6.0–8.0
- Well-drained, moisture-retentive soils
- Avoid saline and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Bt Cotton hybrids: Bollgard-II (most widely grown)
- Non-Bt: MCU-5, Suvin (long staple)
- Desi cotton: G.Cot.10, Jayadhar

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing in summer (30–35 cm)
- 2–3 harrowings for fine tilth
- Apply 10–15 t/ha FYM before sowing
- Form ridges at 90–120 cm spacing

6. SOWING
- Kharif season: April–June
- Seed rate: 2.5 kg/ha (hybrid), 10–12 kg/ha (desi)
- Spacing: 90×60 cm (hybrid), 60×30 cm (desi)
- Depth: 3–4 cm
- Seed treatment: Imidacloprid 70 WS 5 g/kg

7. IRRIGATION
- First irrigation: 30–35 DAS
- Critical stages: squaring, flowering, boll development
- Total requirement: 700–900 mm
- Drip irrigation saves 40–50% water

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 150 kg/ha (3 splits)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Boron: 1 kg/ha foliar spray at flowering

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Quizalofop-ethyl for grass weeds
- Inter-cultivation at 20, 40, 60 DAS

10. HARVESTING
- Pick bolls when fully open (white fluffy fiber)
- 3–4 pickings at 15-day intervals
- Average yield: 2–3 t/ha seed cotton (Bt hybrid)

11. GINNING
- Separate lint from seed at ginning factory
- Lint outturn: 35–38% for Bt hybrids
- Seed used for oil extraction and cattle feed
