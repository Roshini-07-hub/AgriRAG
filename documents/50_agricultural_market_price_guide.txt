AGRICULTURAL MARKET PRICE INFORMATION GUIDE
============================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Agricultural market price information helps farmers make informed decisions about when and where to sell their produce. Access to real-time price information reduces exploitation by middlemen and improves farmer income.

2. MINIMUM SUPPORT PRICE (MSP)

2.1 What is MSP?
- Minimum price guaranteed by government for agricultural produce
- Announced by Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA)
- Based on recommendations of Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices (CACP)
- Covers cost of production + 50% profit margin (A2+FL formula)

2.2 MSP for Major Crops (2023-24)
- Paddy (Common): Rs. 2,183/quintal
- Wheat: Rs. 2,275/quintal
- Maize: Rs. 2,090/quintal
- Soybean: Rs. 4,600/quintal
- Groundnut: Rs. 6,377/quintal
- Cotton (Medium staple): Rs. 6,620/quintal
- Sugarcane (FRP): Rs. 315/quintal
- Tur (Arhar): Rs. 7,000/quintal
- Moong: Rs. 8,558/quintal

3. AGRICULTURAL MARKETS (MANDIS)

3.1 APMC Markets
- Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) regulated markets
- Farmers sell produce through licensed commission agents
- Price discovery through open auction
- Market fee: 1–2% of transaction value

3.2 e-NAM (National Agriculture Market)
- Online trading platform for agricultural commodities
- Launched in 2016; covers 1000+ mandis
- Transparent price discovery
- Direct payment to farmer's bank account
- Website: enam.gov.in

3.3 Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)
- Collective marketing by farmer groups
- Better bargaining power
- Reduced transaction costs
- Access to institutional buyers

4. PRICE INFORMATION SOURCES

4.1 Government Portals
- Agmarknet: agmarknet.nic.in (daily mandi prices)
- e-NAM: enam.gov.in
- Commodity exchanges: NCDEX, MCX

4.2 Mobile Applications
- Kisan Suvidha app (Government of India)
- Agrimarket app
- IFFCO Kisan app
- State agriculture department apps

4.3 SMS Services
- Kisan SMS Portal: 51969
- State-specific SMS services

5. PRICE FORECASTING
- NCDEX futures prices indicate future market trends
- Seasonal price patterns for major crops
- Weather-based price forecasting

6. DIRECT MARKETING CHANNELS
- Farmer markets (Rythu Bazaars, Apni Mandi)
- Contract farming with agri-businesses
- Online platforms: AgriBazaar, DeHaat, Ninjacart
- Export through APEDA registered exporters

7. GRADING AND STANDARDIZATION
- AGMARK certification for quality assurance
- Grading improves price realization by 10–20%
- Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) for processed products

8. STORAGE AND PRICE REALIZATION
- Warehouse Receipt System: Pledge loan against stored produce
- Negotiable Warehouse Receipts (NWR)
- Cold storage for perishables
- Sell when prices are favorable

9. EXPORT OPPORTUNITIES
- APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority)
- Major export commodities: Rice, spices, fruits, vegetables
- Export value: $50+ billion annually
- Organic produce commands premium in export markets

10. FARMER ADVISORY
- Sell in batches rather than all at once
- Use price information before selling
- Consider storage costs vs. price appreciation
- Explore direct marketing to reduce intermediaries
