SOYBEAN PRODUCTION MANUAL
=========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Soybean (Glycine max) is the world's most important oilseed and protein crop. It contains 40% protein and 20% oil. In India, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Rajasthan are major producers.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–30°C
- Rainfall: 600–1000 mm
- Short-day plant; sensitive to photoperiod
- Frost-free growing season of 90–120 days

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained loamy to clay loam soils
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- Good organic matter content
- Avoid waterlogged and acidic soils

4. VARIETIES
- JS-335 (most popular in MP)
- JS-9305, JS-9560
- NRC-37, NRC-86 (ICAR varieties)
- MACS-450 (Maharashtra)

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing after rabi harvest
- 2 harrowings for fine seedbed
- Apply 5 t/ha FYM before sowing
- Broad bed and furrow system recommended

6. SOWING
- Kharif season: June–July
- Seed rate: 70–80 kg/ha
- Spacing: 45×5 cm
- Depth: 3–4 cm
- Seed treatment: Thiram + Carbendazim 2 g/kg + Bradyrhizobium culture

7. IRRIGATION
- Soybean is mostly rainfed
- Supplemental irrigation at flowering and pod filling
- Total requirement: 450–700 mm

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 20 kg/ha (starter dose; Rhizobium fixes 60–80 kg N/ha)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 40 kg/ha (basal)
- Sulphur: 20 kg/ha for oil quality

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Imazethapyr 75 g a.i./ha post-emergence
- Inter-cultivation at 20 and 35 DAS

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest when 95% pods turn brown
- Moisture at harvest: 15–18%
- Dry to 12% for storage
- Average yield: 2–3 t/ha

11. USES
- Soybean oil for cooking and industrial use
- Soy meal for poultry and livestock feed
- Tofu, soy milk, soy sauce
- Biodiesel production
