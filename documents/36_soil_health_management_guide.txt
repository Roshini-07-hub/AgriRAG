SOIL HEALTH MANAGEMENT GUIDE
=============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Soil health refers to the continued capacity of soil to function as a vital living ecosystem that sustains plants, animals, and humans. Healthy soil is the foundation of productive agriculture. Soil degradation is a major threat to food security globally.

2. INDICATORS OF SOIL HEALTH
- Physical: Texture, structure, bulk density, water holding capacity
- Chemical: pH, organic matter, nutrient availability, CEC
- Biological: Microbial biomass, earthworm population, enzyme activity

3. SOIL PHYSICAL PROPERTIES

3.1 Soil Texture
- Sandy soils: Low water retention, good drainage, low fertility
- Clay soils: High water retention, poor drainage, high fertility
- Loamy soils: Ideal balance of sand, silt, and clay

3.2 Soil Structure
- Good structure: Granular, crumbly aggregates
- Poor structure: Compacted, cloddy, or structureless
- Improve with organic matter addition and minimum tillage

3.3 Bulk Density
- Ideal: 1.1–1.4 g/cm³
- High bulk density (>1.6) indicates compaction
- Reduce with deep ploughing and organic matter

4. SOIL CHEMICAL PROPERTIES

4.1 Soil pH
- Ideal range: 6.0–7.0 for most crops
- Acidic soils (pH <6): Apply lime (CaCO3) at 2–4 t/ha
- Alkaline soils (pH >8): Apply gypsum or sulphur

4.2 Organic Matter
- Ideal: >1.5% in tropical soils
- Improves nutrient retention, water holding, and microbial activity
- Increase with FYM, compost, green manure, crop residue incorporation

4.3 Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC)
- Measure of soil's ability to hold nutrients
- Higher CEC = better nutrient retention
- Improve with organic matter and clay addition

5. SOIL BIOLOGICAL PROPERTIES
- Earthworms: 10–20 per m² indicates healthy soil
- Microbial biomass carbon: >200 mg/kg indicates active soil
- Enzyme activity: Dehydrogenase, urease, phosphatase

6. SOIL HEALTH IMPROVEMENT PRACTICES
- Add 10–15 t/ha FYM or compost annually
- Practice crop rotation with legumes
- Minimum tillage to preserve soil structure
- Cover crops to prevent erosion
- Avoid burning crop residues
- Balanced fertilization based on soil test

7. SOIL TESTING
- Test soil every 3 years
- Parameters: pH, EC, organic carbon, N, P, K, micronutrients
- Collect samples from 0–15 cm and 15–30 cm depth
- Send to soil testing laboratory for analysis

8. SOIL CONSERVATION
- Contour bunding to prevent erosion
- Terracing on slopes
- Windbreaks to prevent wind erosion
- Mulching to conserve moisture
