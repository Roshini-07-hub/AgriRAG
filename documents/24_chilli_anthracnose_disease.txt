CHILLI ANTHRACNOSE DISEASE
==========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Anthracnose of chilli is caused by Colletotrichum capsici and C. acutatum. It is one of the most important post-harvest diseases of chilli, causing fruit rot both in field and storage. Yield losses can reach 50% in severe cases.

2. SYMPTOMS
- Fruit symptoms: Circular, sunken, water-soaked lesions on ripe fruits
- Lesions enlarge and turn dark brown to black
- Salmon-pink to orange spore masses (acervuli) in concentric rings
- Infected fruits shrivel and mummify
- Leaf symptoms: Irregular brown spots with yellow halo
- Stem symptoms: Dark brown lesions causing dieback

3. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 25–30°C
- High humidity (>90%) and rainfall
- Warm, wet weather during fruit ripening
- Wounds from insects or mechanical damage
- Prolonged wet season

4. DISEASE CYCLE
- Fungus survives in infected seeds, plant debris, and soil
- Conidia spread by rain splash, wind, and insects
- Infection occurs through wounds and natural openings
- Latent infection in green fruits becomes active at ripening

5. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

5.1 Cultural Control
- Use disease-free certified seeds
- Hot water seed treatment: 52°C for 30 minutes
- Crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops
- Remove and destroy infected fruits and plant debris
- Avoid overhead irrigation; use drip irrigation
- Harvest fruits before full ripening in disease-prone areas

5.2 Chemical Control
- Seed treatment: Thiram 3 g/kg or Carbendazim 2 g/kg
- Foliar spray at fruit development stage:
  * Mancozeb 75 WP at 2.5 g/L
  * Carbendazim 50 WP at 1 g/L
  * Azoxystrobin 23 SC at 1 mL/L
  * Difenoconazole 25 EC at 0.5 mL/L
- Spray at 10–14 day intervals
- Post-harvest treatment: Carbendazim 0.1% dip

5.3 Biological Control
- Trichoderma viride seed treatment
- Bacillus subtilis foliar spray

6. RESISTANT VARIETIES
- CA-960, Pusa Jwala (tolerant)
- Hybrid varieties with field tolerance

7. POST-HARVEST MANAGEMENT
- Harvest at correct maturity stage
- Avoid mechanical damage during harvest
- Store at 8–10°C with 90–95% humidity
- Fungicide dip before storage
