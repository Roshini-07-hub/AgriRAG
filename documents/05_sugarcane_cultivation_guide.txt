SUGARCANE CULTIVATION GUIDE
===========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum) is the primary source of sugar and ethanol. India is the second largest producer of sugarcane after Brazil. Major growing states: Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–35°C; warm humid climate
- Rainfall: 1500–2500 mm or irrigation equivalent
- Long growing season: 10–18 months
- Frost damages the crop severely

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Deep, well-drained loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- High organic matter content
- Avoid waterlogged and saline soils

4. VARIETIES
- Early maturing (10–11 months): CoC-671, Co-86032
- Mid-late (12–14 months): Co-0238, CoJ-64
- Ratoon varieties: CoS-8436, CoSe-92423

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing to 30–35 cm
- Subsoiling every 3–4 years
- Apply 25 t/ha FYM before planting
- Form furrows at 90 cm spacing

6. PLANTING
- Planting time: February–March (spring), October–November (autumn)
- Use 3-budded setts as planting material
- Seed rate: 35,000–40,000 setts/ha
- Treat setts with Carbendazim 0.1% solution
- Plant in furrows at 5–7 cm depth

7. IRRIGATION
- First irrigation immediately after planting
- Irrigate every 7–10 days in summer
- Total water requirement: 1500–2000 mm
- Drip irrigation increases yield by 20–30%

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 250–300 kg/ha (3–4 splits)
- Phosphorus: 80 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 120 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Trash mulching conserves moisture and adds organic matter

9. RATOON MANAGEMENT
- Retain ratoon after main crop harvest
- Gap filling within 30 days
- Apply 20% extra nitrogen for ratoon
- Ratoon yield: 70–80% of plant crop

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest at 10–18 months depending on variety
- Cane juice sucrose content: 12–14%
- Cut at ground level to protect ratoon buds
- Average yield: 70–100 t/ha

11. BY-PRODUCTS
- Bagasse: Used for paper and energy
- Molasses: Used for ethanol production
- Press mud: Used as organic manure
