RICE CULTIVATION GUIDE
======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Rice (Oryza sativa) is the staple food crop for more than half the world's population. It is primarily grown in tropical and subtropical regions with abundant water supply. India is the second largest producer of rice globally.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–35°C during growing season
- Rainfall: 1000–2000 mm annually
- Humidity: High humidity favors growth
- Altitude: Best grown below 2000 m above sea level

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Soil type: Heavy clay or clay loam soils with good water retention
- pH range: 5.5–7.0
- Avoid sandy or well-drained soils
- Alluvial soils of river deltas are ideal

4. VARIETIES
- Short duration (90–110 days): IR-36, IR-64, Jaya
- Medium duration (120–140 days): Swarna, MTU-7029
- Long duration (150–180 days): Basmati, Pusa Basmati-1
- Hybrid varieties: KRH-2, DRRH-2

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Plough the field 2–3 times to a depth of 15–20 cm
- Puddle the field thoroughly to reduce percolation
- Level the field for uniform water distribution
- Apply 10–15 tonnes/ha of farmyard manure before final ploughing

6. NURSERY PREPARATION
- Prepare raised nursery beds of 1 m width
- Sow pre-germinated seeds at 50 kg/ha
- Maintain 2–3 cm water in nursery
- Seedlings ready for transplanting in 20–25 days

7. TRANSPLANTING
- Transplant 2–3 seedlings per hill at 20×15 cm spacing
- Maintain 5 cm water depth after transplanting
- Gap filling within 7 days of transplanting

8. WATER MANAGEMENT
- Maintain 5 cm standing water during vegetative stage
- Drain field 10 days before harvest
- Critical stages: tillering, panicle initiation, flowering
- Total water requirement: 1200–1500 mm per crop

9. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 120 kg/ha (split in 3 doses)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal dose)
- Potassium: 60 kg/ha (basal dose)
- Zinc sulphate: 25 kg/ha if deficiency observed

10. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pre-emergence herbicide: Butachlor 1.5 kg a.i./ha at 3–5 DAT
- Post-emergence: 2,4-D sodium salt 0.5 kg a.i./ha at 20–25 DAT
- Manual weeding at 20 and 40 days after transplanting

11. HARVESTING
- Harvest when 80–85% grains turn golden yellow
- Moisture content at harvest: 20–22%
- Use combine harvester or manual harvesting
- Average yield: 4–6 tonnes/ha under irrigated conditions

12. POST-HARVEST
- Dry grains to 14% moisture for safe storage
- Store in clean, dry, well-ventilated godowns
- Use hermetic bags to prevent storage pests
