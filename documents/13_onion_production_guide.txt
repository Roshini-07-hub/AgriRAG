ONION PRODUCTION GUIDE
======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Onion (Allium cepa) is an important vegetable and spice crop. India is the second largest producer globally. Maharashtra, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, and Gujarat are major producing states.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 13–24°C for bulb development
- Cool weather during vegetative growth
- Warm dry weather at bulb maturity
- Long days (13–14 hours) promote bulbing

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.0
- Rich in organic matter
- Avoid heavy clay and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Kharif: Agrifound Dark Red, Baswant-780
- Rabi: Pusa Red, Nasik Red, N-2-4-1
- White onion: Pusa White Flat, Udaipur-102

5. NURSERY PREPARATION
- Prepare raised beds 1 m wide
- Sow seeds at 8–10 g/m²
- Seedlings ready in 40–45 days
- Treat with Carbendazim 1 g/L before transplanting

6. TRANSPLANTING
- Transplant 6–8 week old seedlings
- Spacing: 15×10 cm
- Transplant in evening
- Water immediately after transplanting

7. IRRIGATION
- Irrigate every 7–10 days
- Stop irrigation 15 days before harvest
- Total requirement: 350–500 mm
- Drip irrigation saves 30–40% water

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 100 kg/ha (3 splits)
- Phosphorus: 50 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 50 kg/ha (basal)
- Sulphur: 20 kg/ha for pungency and storage quality

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Hand weeding at 30 and 50 DAT
- Avoid deep cultivation near bulbs

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest when 50–75% tops fall over
- Cure in field for 3–5 days
- Dry in shade for 7–10 days
- Average yield: 20–30 t/ha

11. STORAGE
- Store in well-ventilated cool dry place
- Avoid moisture and direct sunlight
- Storage life: 3–6 months at ambient conditions
- Cold storage at 0–2°C extends shelf life to 6–8 months
