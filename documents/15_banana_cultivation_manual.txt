BANANA CULTIVATION MANUAL
=========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Banana (Musa spp.) is the most important fruit crop in India. It is grown throughout the year and is a rich source of carbohydrates, potassium, and vitamins. Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Maharashtra are major producers.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–35°C optimal
- Rainfall: 1200–2500 mm or irrigation equivalent
- High humidity (75–85%) promotes growth
- Sensitive to frost, strong winds, and waterlogging

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Deep, well-drained loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- High organic matter content
- Avoid saline, alkaline, and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Cavendish group: Grand Naine (most popular), Robusta
- Poovan: Rasthali, Karpooravalli
- Nendran: Cooking banana (Kerala)
- Red banana: Lal Velchi

5. PLANTING MATERIAL
- Suckers: Sword suckers preferred
- Tissue culture plants: Disease-free, uniform
- Rhizome bits: Used for large-scale planting

6. PLANTING
- Planting time: June–July or February–March
- Pit size: 60×60×60 cm
- Spacing: 1.8×1.8 m (Grand Naine), 2.1×2.1 m (tall varieties)
- Fill pit with topsoil + FYM (10 kg) + Neem cake (1 kg)
- Plant at 15–20 cm depth

7. IRRIGATION
- Drip irrigation: 8–10 L/plant/day
- Total requirement: 1800–2200 mm/year
- Critical stages: shooting, bunch development
- Avoid waterlogging

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 200 g/plant/year (4 splits)
- Phosphorus: 100 g/plant/year (2 splits)
- Potassium: 300 g/plant/year (4 splits)
- Potassium is critical for bunch weight and quality

9. DESUCKERING AND PROPPING
- Remove all suckers except one ratoon
- Prop plants with bamboo poles at shooting stage
- Remove male bud after last hand develops

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest 12–15 months after planting
- Harvest when fingers are 75% full (green stage)
- Average bunch weight: 25–35 kg
- Yield: 40–60 t/ha

11. POST-HARVEST
- Ripen with ethylene (100 ppm) for 24–48 hours
- Store at 13–14°C for 2–3 weeks
- Export quality: Grade A (>200 g/finger)
