ARMYWORM IDENTIFICATION AND CONTROL
=====================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Armyworms are caterpillars of moths in the family Noctuidae. They are called armyworms because they move in large numbers like an army. Major species: Common armyworm (Mythimna separata), Beet armyworm (Spodoptera exigua), Tobacco caterpillar (Spodoptera litura).

2. COMMON ARMYWORM (Mythimna separata)

2.1 Identification
- Adult: Pale brown moth with white spot on forewing
- Larvae: Pale green to brown with dark stripes, 35–40 mm

2.2 Host Crops
- Rice, wheat, maize, sorghum, sugarcane

2.3 Damage
- Defoliation of leaves
- Cutting of stems at base
- Moves in large groups, can devastate fields overnight

3. TOBACCO CATERPILLAR (Spodoptera litura)

3.1 Identification
- Adult: Brown moth with wavy white lines on forewing
- Larvae: Dark brown with yellow stripes, 35–40 mm

3.2 Host Crops
- Groundnut, tobacco, cotton, vegetables

3.3 Damage
- Skeletonization of leaves
- Complete defoliation in severe cases
- Nocturnal feeding

4. BEET ARMYWORM (Spodoptera exigua)

4.1 Damage
- Feeds on leaves, flowers, and fruits
- Window pane feeding on young leaves

5. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL
- 4–5 larvae per m² for armyworm
- 2 egg masses per m² for tobacco caterpillar

6. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

6.1 Cultural Control
- Deep ploughing to expose pupae
- Remove egg masses and young larvae by hand
- Light traps for adult monitoring
- Crop rotation

6.2 Biological Control
- NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus): 250 LE/ha for Spodoptera
- Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki: 1 kg/ha
- Trichogramma chilonis: Release at egg laying
- Chrysoperla carnea: Predator

6.3 Chemical Control
- Emamectin benzoate 5 SG at 0.4 g/L
- Spinosad 45 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Indoxacarb 14.5 SC at 1 mL/L
- Quinalphos 25 EC at 2 mL/L

7. MONITORING
- Light traps: 1 per 5 hectares
- Pheromone traps: 5 per hectare
- Regular field scouting at night (nocturnal feeders)
