GROUNDNUT FARMING GUIDE
=======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Groundnut (Arachis hypogaea), also called peanut, is the most important oilseed crop in India. It is rich in protein (25–28%) and oil (48–50%). Major producing states: Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 25–30°C optimal
- Rainfall: 500–1000 mm
- Warm growing season of 90–130 days
- Dry weather at pod maturity is essential

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Light sandy loam to loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.0
- Well-drained, loose soils for peg penetration
- Avoid heavy clay and waterlogged soils
- Calcium-rich soils improve pod filling

4. VARIETIES
- Bunch type: TAG-24, GG-20, TG-37A
- Spreading type: Kadiri-3, TMV-2
- Virginia type: GPBD-4, VRI-2

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing to 25–30 cm
- 2–3 harrowings for fine tilth
- Apply 5 t/ha FYM before sowing
- Gypsum application: 500 kg/ha at pegging stage

6. SOWING
- Kharif: June–July; Rabi: November–December
- Seed rate: 100–120 kg/ha (shelled)
- Spacing: 30×10 cm
- Depth: 5–6 cm
- Seed treatment: Thiram 3 g/kg + Rhizobium culture

7. IRRIGATION
- Critical stages: flowering, pegging, pod development
- Total requirement: 400–500 mm
- Avoid waterlogging during pod filling

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 20 kg/ha (basal, Rhizobium fixes rest)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 40 kg/ha (basal)
- Gypsum: 500 kg/ha at pegging for calcium supply

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Imazethapyr 75 g a.i./ha post-emergence
- Hand weeding at 20 and 40 DAS

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest when leaves turn yellow and pods show dark veins
- Maturity: 90–130 days depending on variety
- Dry pods to 8–10% moisture
- Average yield: 2–3 t/ha pods

11. USES
- Oil extraction (48–50% oil content)
- Peanut butter, roasted snacks
- Groundnut cake as cattle feed
- Groundnut oil for cooking
