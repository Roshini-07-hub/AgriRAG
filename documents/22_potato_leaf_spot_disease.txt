POTATO LEAF SPOT DISEASE
========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Potato leaf spot diseases include Early Blight (Alternaria solani) and Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans). These are the most important foliar diseases of potato causing significant yield and quality losses.

2. EARLY BLIGHT SYMPTOMS
- Dark brown circular spots with concentric rings on leaves
- Yellow halo around lesions
- Lesions first appear on older lower leaves
- Severe defoliation reduces tuber yield
- Tuber lesions: Dark, sunken, corky spots

3. LATE BLIGHT SYMPTOMS
- Water-soaked pale green lesions rapidly turning brown-black
- White cottony growth on underside of leaves
- Entire plant collapses within days
- Tuber blight: Brown, firm, granular rot extending into flesh

4. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS FOR EARLY BLIGHT
- Temperature: 24–29°C
- High humidity and leaf wetness
- Nutritional stress

5. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS FOR LATE BLIGHT
- Temperature: 10–20°C
- Relative humidity >90%
- Cool, cloudy, foggy weather

6. MANAGEMENT OF EARLY BLIGHT

6.1 Chemical Control
- Mancozeb 75 WP at 2.5 g/L
- Chlorothalonil 75 WP at 2 g/L
- Iprodione 50 WP at 1.5 g/L
- Spray at 10–14 day intervals

7. MANAGEMENT OF LATE BLIGHT

7.1 Chemical Control
- Metalaxyl + Mancozeb at 2.5 g/L (curative)
- Cymoxanil + Mancozeb at 2.5 g/L
- Dimethomorph 50 WP at 1 g/L
- Spray at 5–7 day intervals during outbreak

8. COMMON CULTURAL PRACTICES
- Use certified disease-free seed tubers
- Treat seed tubers with Mancozeb 0.25% before planting
- Crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops
- Avoid overhead irrigation
- Remove and destroy infected plant material
- Earthing up prevents tuber infection

9. RESISTANT VARIETIES
- Kufri Jyoti (tolerant to late blight)
- Kufri Giriraj (hills, late blight resistant)
- Kufri Chipsona-3 (processing, tolerant)

10. INTEGRATED MANAGEMENT
- Combine resistant varieties + cultural practices + timely fungicide application
- Monitor weather conditions for disease forecasting
- Economic threshold: Spray at first symptom appearance
