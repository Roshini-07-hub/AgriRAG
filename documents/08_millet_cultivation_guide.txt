MILLET CULTIVATION GUIDE
========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Millets are small-seeded grasses grown as food and fodder crops. Major millets include Pearl millet (Bajra), Sorghum (Jowar), Finger millet (Ragi), and Foxtail millet. They are drought-tolerant, nutritious, and suited to dryland farming.

2. PEARL MILLET (BAJRA)
- Climate: Hot, dry; temperature 25–35°C
- Rainfall: 300–500 mm
- Soil: Sandy loam, pH 6.0–8.0
- Varieties: HHB-67, GHB-558, Pusa-322
- Seed rate: 4–5 kg/ha
- Spacing: 45×15 cm
- Duration: 65–90 days
- Yield: 2–3 t/ha grain

3. SORGHUM (JOWAR)
- Climate: Semi-arid; temperature 26–34°C
- Rainfall: 400–600 mm
- Soil: Deep black cotton soils, pH 6.5–7.5
- Varieties: CSH-16, CSH-25, SPV-462
- Seed rate: 10–12 kg/ha
- Spacing: 45×15 cm
- Duration: 100–120 days
- Yield: 3–4 t/ha grain

4. FINGER MILLET (RAGI)
- Climate: Warm humid; temperature 20–30°C
- Rainfall: 700–1000 mm
- Soil: Red loamy soils, pH 5.5–7.0
- Varieties: GPU-28, MR-6, Indaf-5
- Seed rate: 10 kg/ha (transplanted)
- Spacing: 22.5×10 cm
- Duration: 90–130 days
- Yield: 2–3 t/ha

5. COMMON LAND PREPARATION
- Plough to 20–25 cm depth
- 2 harrowings for fine tilth
- Apply 5 t/ha FYM before sowing

6. FERTILIZER APPLICATION (PEARL MILLET)
- Nitrogen: 80 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Phosphorus: 40 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 40 kg/ha (basal)

7. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Atrazine 0.5 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence (sorghum)
- Hand weeding at 20 and 40 DAS

8. HARVESTING
- Harvest when grain is hard and moisture is 20–25%
- Dry to 12% for storage
- Millets store well for 2–3 years

9. NUTRITIONAL VALUE
- Ragi: Highest calcium content (344 mg/100g)
- Bajra: Rich in iron and zinc
- Jowar: Gluten-free, good for diabetics
- All millets: High fiber, low glycemic index
