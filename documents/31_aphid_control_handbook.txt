APHID CONTROL HANDBOOK
======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Aphids (Order: Hemiptera, Family: Aphididae) are small soft-bodied insects that suck plant sap. They are among the most destructive pests of agriculture, causing direct damage and transmitting plant viruses. Common species: Aphis gossypii (cotton/melon aphid), Myzus persicae (green peach aphid), Lipaphis erysimi (mustard aphid).

2. IDENTIFICATION
- Small (1–3 mm), soft-bodied, pear-shaped
- Colors: Green, yellow, black, brown, pink
- Two cornicles (tubes) on abdomen
- Winged and wingless forms
- Found in colonies on young shoots and underside of leaves

3. DAMAGE SYMPTOMS
- Curling and distortion of young leaves
- Yellowing and stunting of plants
- Honeydew secretion leading to sooty mold
- Virus transmission: Potato virus Y, Cucumber mosaic virus, Barley yellow dwarf virus
- Ant attendance (ants protect aphids for honeydew)

4. LIFE CYCLE
- Rapid reproduction: 1 female produces 50–100 offspring
- Parthenogenesis (asexual reproduction) in summer
- Sexual reproduction in autumn
- 10–20 generations per year
- Winged forms disperse to new plants

5. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Mild temperatures (15–25°C)
- Moderate humidity
- Tender plant growth (excess nitrogen)
- Absence of natural enemies

6. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL
- Mustard: 26 aphids per plant
- Wheat: 10–15 aphids per tiller
- Vegetables: 10–15 per leaf
- Cotton: 10–15 per leaf

7. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

7.1 Cultural Control
- Avoid excess nitrogen application
- Remove and destroy heavily infested plant parts
- Reflective silver mulch repels aphids
- Intercropping with coriander, dill (attract natural enemies)

7.2 Biological Control
- Coccinella septempunctata (ladybird beetle): Major predator
- Chrysoperla carnea (lacewing): Predator
- Aphidius colemani: Parasitoid wasp
- Syrphid flies: Larvae feed on aphids
- Conserve natural enemies by avoiding broad-spectrum pesticides

7.3 Chemical Control
- Spray with water jet to dislodge aphids
- Neem-based insecticides: NSKE 5% or Neem oil 3%
- Insecticidal soap: 2% solution
- Chemical insecticides:
  * Imidacloprid 17.8 SL at 0.5 mL/L
  * Thiamethoxam 25 WG at 0.3 g/L
  * Dimethoate 30 EC at 2 mL/L
  * Malathion 50 EC at 2 mL/L

8. VIRUS MANAGEMENT
- Control aphid vectors to prevent virus spread
- Use virus-free planting material
- Mineral oil sprays reduce virus transmission
