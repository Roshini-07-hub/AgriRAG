SPRINKLER IRRIGATION MANUAL
============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Sprinkler irrigation simulates rainfall by distributing water through a system of pipes and sprinkler heads. It is suitable for uneven terrain, sandy soils, and crops that benefit from overhead watering. Water saving: 20–30% compared to flood irrigation.

2. TYPES OF SPRINKLER SYSTEMS

2.1 Portable System
- Aluminum or PVC pipes that can be moved
- Suitable for small farms
- Lower cost but labor-intensive

2.2 Semi-Permanent System
- Main lines are permanent; laterals are portable
- Suitable for medium farms

2.3 Permanent System
- All pipes are buried underground
- Suitable for large farms and orchards
- High initial cost but low labor

2.4 Center Pivot System
- Rotating arm irrigates circular area
- Suitable for large flat fields
- Highly automated

2.5 Micro-Sprinkler System
- Low-volume sprinklers for orchards
- Coverage: 2–4 m diameter

3. COMPONENTS
- Pump and motor
- Main line and sub-main line
- Lateral pipes with risers
- Sprinkler heads (rotating or fixed)
- Pressure gauges and control valves

4. DESIGN PARAMETERS
- Operating pressure: 2.0–3.5 kg/cm²
- Application rate: 5–15 mm/hour
- Sprinkler spacing: 12×12 m to 18×18 m
- Uniformity coefficient: >85%

5. SUITABLE CROPS
- Wheat, groundnut, vegetables, fodder crops
- Orchards: Mango, citrus, guava
- Nurseries and lawns
- Not suitable for tall crops (maize, sugarcane)

6. ADVANTAGES
- Suitable for uneven terrain
- Reduces soil erosion
- Can be used for frost protection
- Suitable for germination of small seeds
- Reduces weed growth compared to flood irrigation

7. DISADVANTAGES
- High initial cost
- Wind affects distribution uniformity
- Wet foliage promotes fungal diseases
- Not suitable for saline water

8. OPERATION
- Operate during early morning or evening (reduce evaporation)
- Avoid operation during windy conditions
- Apply water at rate less than soil infiltration rate
- Irrigate to field capacity

9. MAINTENANCE
- Clean nozzles regularly
- Check for leaks in pipes
- Lubricate rotating heads
- Flush system before seasonal use

10. GOVERNMENT SUBSIDY
- PMKSY subsidy: 55% for small/marginal farmers
- Apply through state agriculture department
- Installation cost: Rs. 30,000–80,000/ha
