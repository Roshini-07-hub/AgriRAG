SOIL TESTING PROCEDURES MANUAL
================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Soil testing is the analysis of soil samples to determine nutrient status, pH, and other properties. It is the foundation of scientific fertilizer recommendations. Regular soil testing every 3 years is recommended for all agricultural fields.

2. SOIL SAMPLING

2.1 When to Sample
- Before sowing or planting
- After harvest of previous crop
- Avoid sampling immediately after fertilizer application

2.2 Sampling Pattern
- Zigzag pattern across the field
- Collect 10–15 sub-samples per field
- Composite into one representative sample

2.3 Sampling Depth
- Surface soil: 0–15 cm (for most crops)
- Subsoil: 15–30 cm (for deep-rooted crops)
- Separate samples for different soil types in same field

2.4 Sampling Tools
- Soil auger or core sampler
- Clean plastic bucket
- Avoid galvanized or iron tools (contaminate micronutrient samples)

3. SAMPLE PREPARATION
- Air dry sample in shade (avoid direct sunlight)
- Crush clods and remove stones, roots, debris
- Grind and pass through 2 mm sieve
- Collect 500 g for analysis

4. SOIL ANALYSIS PARAMETERS

4.1 Basic Parameters
- Soil pH: Measured in 1:2.5 soil:water suspension
- Electrical Conductivity (EC): Measures salinity
- Organic Carbon: Walkley-Black method
- Available Nitrogen: Alkaline permanganate method
- Available Phosphorus: Olsen or Bray method
- Available Potassium: Ammonium acetate extraction

4.2 Micronutrient Analysis
- DTPA extraction for Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu
- Hot water extraction for Boron
- Colorimetric or AAS analysis

4.3 Physical Analysis
- Texture: Hydrometer method
- Bulk density: Core method
- Water holding capacity

5. INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS

5.1 Soil pH
- <5.5: Very acidic — apply lime
- 5.5–6.5: Slightly acidic — ideal for most crops
- 6.5–7.5: Neutral — ideal
- 7.5–8.5: Alkaline — apply gypsum or sulphur
- >8.5: Highly alkaline — reclamation needed

5.2 Organic Carbon
- <0.5%: Very low — apply heavy doses of organic manure
- 0.5–0.75%: Low
- 0.75–1.0%: Medium
- >1.0%: High

5.3 Available Nutrients (kg/ha)
- Nitrogen: Low <280, Medium 280–560, High >560
- Phosphorus: Low <10, Medium 10–25, High >25
- Potassium: Low <108, Medium 108–280, High >280

6. SOIL TESTING LABORATORIES
- State Department of Agriculture labs
- ICAR institute labs
- Private accredited labs
- Mobile soil testing vans (government)

7. SOIL HEALTH CARD
- Government of India provides Soil Health Cards to farmers
- Contains soil test results and fertilizer recommendations
- Updated every 3 years
- Available online at soilhealth.dac.gov.in
