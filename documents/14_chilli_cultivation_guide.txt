CHILLI CULTIVATION GUIDE
========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Chilli (Capsicum annuum) is an important spice and vegetable crop. India is the largest producer and exporter of chilli. Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh are major producers.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–30°C optimal
- Warm humid climate during vegetative growth
- Dry weather at fruit maturity
- Sensitive to frost and waterlogging

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.0
- Rich in organic matter
- Avoid heavy clay and saline soils

4. VARIETIES
- Pungent: LCA-206, G-4, Byadgi Kaddi
- Mild: California Wonder, Yolo Wonder (capsicum)
- Hybrid: Arka Meghna, Arka Sweta
- Paprika: Paprika Selection-12

5. NURSERY PREPARATION
- Prepare raised beds 1 m wide
- Sow seeds at 500 g/ha
- Seedlings ready in 30–35 days
- Treat with Imidacloprid 0.5 mL/L before transplanting

6. TRANSPLANTING
- Transplant 30–35 day old seedlings
- Spacing: 60×45 cm
- Transplant in evening
- Water immediately after transplanting

7. IRRIGATION
- Drip irrigation preferred
- Critical stages: flowering, fruit set, fruit development
- Total requirement: 500–700 mm
- Avoid water stress during flowering

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 120 kg/ha (3 splits)
- Phosphorus: 60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 60 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Boron: 0.2% foliar spray at flowering

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pendimethalin 1.0 kg a.i./ha pre-emergence
- Hand weeding at 30 and 60 DAT
- Mulching with black polythene reduces weeds

10. HARVESTING
- Green chilli: 60–70 days after transplanting
- Red dry chilli: 90–120 days after transplanting
- Harvest at 4–5 day intervals
- Average yield: 15–25 t/ha green; 2–3 t/ha dry

11. DRYING AND STORAGE
- Sun dry red chillies to 8–10% moisture
- Store in gunny bags in cool dry place
- Capsaicin content determines pungency (Scoville units)
