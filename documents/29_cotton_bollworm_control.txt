COTTON BOLLWORM CONTROL
=======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Bollworms are the most destructive pests of cotton. Three major species: American bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera), Pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella), and Spotted bollworm (Earias vittella). They cause yield losses of 30–60%.

2. AMERICAN BOLLWORM (Helicoverpa armigera)

2.1 Identification
- Adult: Stout moth, yellowish-brown with dark spots
- Larvae: Variable color (green, brown, black); 35–40 mm long
- Eggs: Dome-shaped, ribbed, creamy white

2.2 Damage
- Feeds on squares, flowers, and bolls
- Entry hole with frass at boll surface
- Boll rotting and premature opening

3. PINK BOLLWORM (Pectinophora gossypiella)

3.1 Identification
- Adult: Small moth, grayish-brown
- Larvae: Pink colored, 12–15 mm long
- Eggs: Flat, oval, white

3.2 Damage
- Larvae feed inside bolls on seeds
- Rosette flower (locking of petals)
- Lint staining and reduced fiber quality

4. SPOTTED BOLLWORM (Earias vittella)

4.1 Identification
- Adult: Small moth with green and white wings
- Larvae: Yellowish with dark spots

4.2 Damage
- Shoots: Dead heart in young plants
- Bolls: Entry hole with frass

5. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL
- American bollworm: 5 larvae per 10 plants
- Pink bollworm: 5% rosette flowers or 10% infested bolls
- Spotted bollworm: 5% dead hearts

6. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

6.1 Cultural Control
- Early sowing to avoid peak pest season
- Destroy crop residues after harvest
- Deep summer ploughing to expose pupae
- Avoid ratoon cotton

6.2 Biological Control
- Trichogramma chilonis: Release 1,50,000 eggs/ha at egg laying
- NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus): 250 LE/ha
- Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki: 1 kg/ha
- Chrysoperla carnea: Release 50,000 larvae/ha

6.3 Chemical Control
- Rotate insecticides to prevent resistance:
  * Emamectin benzoate 5 SG at 0.4 g/L
  * Spinosad 45 SC at 0.3 mL/L
  * Indoxacarb 14.5 SC at 1 mL/L
  * Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC at 0.3 mL/L
- Avoid pyrethroids for Helicoverpa (resistance)

6.4 Bt Cotton
- Bollgard-II expresses Cry1Ac + Cry2Ab proteins
- Effective against American and spotted bollworm
- Pink bollworm developing resistance to Bt
