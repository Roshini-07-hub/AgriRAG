FALL ARMYWORM MANAGEMENT GUIDE
================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Fall Armyworm (FAW), Spodoptera frugiperda, is an invasive pest native to the Americas. It was first detected in Africa in 2016 and in India (Karnataka) in 2018. It is a highly polyphagous pest that primarily attacks maize but also damages sorghum, wheat, sugarcane, and vegetables.

2. IDENTIFICATION
- Adult: Moth with 32–40 mm wingspan; male has white spots on forewing
- Larvae: 6 instars; early instars pale green; late instars brown with stripes
- Key identification: Inverted Y on head capsule; 4 black spots in square pattern on 8th abdominal segment
- Eggs: Dome-shaped, laid in masses of 100–200, covered with scales

3. DAMAGE SYMPTOMS
- Maize: Ragged feeding on leaves; frass in whorl; window pane feeding
- Characteristic frass (sawdust-like) in whorl
- Feeding on tassel and ear
- Severe infestation: Complete defoliation
- Damage rating scale: 0 (no damage) to 9 (complete destruction)

4. LIFE CYCLE
- Egg: 2–3 days
- Larval stage: 14–22 days (6 instars)
- Pupal stage: 8–9 days
- Adult: 10–21 days
- Total life cycle: 30–50 days
- Multiple generations per year; no diapause

5. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 25–35°C
- Humid conditions
- Maize at whorl stage (most susceptible)
- Absence of natural enemies

6. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL
- 5–10% plants with fresh damage
- 1–2 larvae per plant at whorl stage

7. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

7.1 Cultural Control
- Early sowing to avoid peak FAW season
- Intercropping maize with beans or cowpea
- Push-pull technology: Desmodium as intercrop, Napier grass as border
- Sand or ash in whorl to kill young larvae
- Remove and destroy egg masses

7.2 Biological Control
- Trichogramma pretiosum: Release at egg laying
- Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki: 1 kg/ha in whorl
- Metarhizium rileyi: Entomopathogenic fungus
- Spodoptera frugiperda NPV: 250 LE/ha
- Telenomus remus: Egg parasitoid

7.3 Chemical Control
- Apply in whorl at 5% damage:
  * Emamectin benzoate 5 SG at 0.4 g/L
  * Spinetoram 11.7 SC at 0.5 mL/L
  * Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC at 0.3 mL/L
  * Thiamethoxam + Lambda-cyhalothrin at 0.5 mL/L
- Add sand to spray for better whorl penetration

7.4 Pheromone Traps
- FAW-specific pheromone lures
- 5 traps per hectare for monitoring
- Mass trapping with funnel traps

8. QUARANTINE MEASURES
- Inspect imported maize seed and plant material
- Report new infestations to plant protection authorities
