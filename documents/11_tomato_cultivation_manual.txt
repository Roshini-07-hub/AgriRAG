TOMATO CULTIVATION MANUAL
=========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is the most important vegetable crop worldwide. It is rich in lycopene, vitamins A and C. India produces about 20 million tonnes annually. Major states: Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Odisha.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20–27°C optimal; 15–35°C range
- Sensitive to frost and extreme heat
- Requires 6–8 hours of sunlight daily
- Humidity above 80% promotes fungal diseases

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.0
- Rich in organic matter
- Avoid heavy clay and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Hybrid: Arka Rakshak, Pusa Hybrid-1, Naveen
- Open-pollinated: Pusa Ruby, Arka Vikas, CO-3
- Cherry tomato: Arka Bhanu
- Processing: Arka Saurabh

5. NURSERY PREPARATION
- Prepare raised beds 1 m wide, 15 cm high
- Sow seeds at 5 g/m² in lines 10 cm apart
- Cover with fine soil and mulch
- Seedlings ready in 25–30 days
- Treat nursery with Carbendazim 1 g/L

6. TRANSPLANTING
- Transplant 25–30 day old seedlings
- Spacing: 60×45 cm (staked), 75×60 cm (unstaked)
- Transplant in evening or on cloudy day
- Water immediately after transplanting

7. IRRIGATION
- Drip irrigation preferred
- Critical stages: flowering, fruit set, fruit development
- Total requirement: 400–600 mm
- Avoid water stress and excess moisture

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 120 kg/ha (3 splits)
- Phosphorus: 80 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 80 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Calcium nitrate: 1% foliar spray to prevent blossom end rot

9. STAKING AND PRUNING
- Stake plants at 30–40 cm height
- Remove suckers for indeterminate varieties
- Pinch growing tip after 4–5 fruit clusters

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest at mature green to red stage
- First harvest: 60–70 days after transplanting
- Harvesting interval: 4–5 days
- Average yield: 40–60 t/ha (hybrid)

11. POST-HARVEST
- Store at 10–13°C for 2–3 weeks
- Avoid ethylene exposure during storage
- Process into ketchup, puree, paste
