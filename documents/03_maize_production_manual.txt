MAIZE PRODUCTION MANUAL
=======================

1. INTRODUCTION
Maize (Zea mays) is the third most important cereal crop globally. It is used for food, feed, and industrial purposes. In India, maize is grown in both kharif and rabi seasons across Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, and Rajasthan.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 18–32°C optimal
- Rainfall: 500–750 mm well-distributed
- Sensitive to frost and waterlogging
- Requires 110–120 frost-free days

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained loamy soils
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- Deep soils with high organic matter
- Avoid heavy clay and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Hybrid: DHM-117, HQPM-1 (quality protein maize)
- Composite: Navjot, Amber
- Baby corn: VL-78, HM-4
- Sweet corn: Madhuri, WNMH-2

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing to 25–30 cm
- 2 harrowings for fine seedbed
- Apply 10 t/ha FYM at last ploughing
- Form ridges and furrows for row planting

6. SOWING
- Kharif: June–July; Rabi: October–November
- Seed rate: 20–25 kg/ha (hybrids), 15–20 kg/ha
- Spacing: 60×20 cm (kharif), 60×15 cm (rabi)
- Depth: 4–5 cm
- Seed treatment: Thiram + Carbendazim 2 g/kg

7. IRRIGATION
- Critical stages: knee-high, tasseling, silking, grain filling
- Total requirement: 400–600 mm
- Avoid waterlogging at any stage

8. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 150–180 kg/ha (3 splits)
- Phosphorus: 70 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 70 kg/ha (basal)
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha if deficient

9. WEED MANAGEMENT
- Atrazine 1.0 kg a.i./ha as pre-emergence
- Tembotrione post-emergence at 3–4 leaf stage
- Inter-cultivation at 20 and 35 DAS

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest at 30–35% grain moisture for mechanical harvest
- Dry to 12–14% for storage
- Average yield: 5–8 t/ha hybrid varieties

11. USES
- Food: Flour, porridge, tortillas
- Feed: Poultry and livestock
- Industrial: Starch, ethanol, corn syrup
