PULSES CULTIVATION GUIDE
========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Pulses are leguminous crops grown for their edible seeds. They are rich in protein (20–25%) and fix atmospheric nitrogen. Major pulses in India: Chickpea (Gram), Pigeon pea (Tur/Arhar), Lentil (Masoor), Mung bean (Moong), Black gram (Urad).

2. CHICKPEA (GRAM)
- Season: Rabi (October–November sowing)
- Climate: Cool dry weather; 15–25°C
- Soil: Sandy loam to clay loam, pH 6.0–8.0
- Varieties: JG-11, Pusa-362, KAK-2
- Seed rate: 80–100 kg/ha
- Spacing: 30×10 cm
- Duration: 90–110 days
- Yield: 1.5–2.5 t/ha

3. PIGEON PEA (TUR/ARHAR)
- Season: Kharif (June–July sowing)
- Climate: Warm semi-arid; 25–35°C
- Soil: Well-drained loamy soils, pH 6.0–7.5
- Varieties: ICPL-87119, Asha, Maruti
- Seed rate: 15–20 kg/ha
- Spacing: 90×30 cm (long duration), 60×20 cm (short)
- Duration: 120–270 days
- Yield: 1.5–2.0 t/ha

4. LENTIL (MASOOR)
- Season: Rabi (October–November)
- Climate: Cool dry; 15–25°C
- Soil: Loamy soils, pH 6.0–8.0
- Varieties: Pant L-406, K-75, DPL-62
- Seed rate: 40–50 kg/ha
- Spacing: 25×5 cm
- Duration: 90–120 days
- Yield: 1.0–1.5 t/ha

5. MUNG BEAN (MOONG)
- Season: Kharif and summer
- Climate: Warm humid; 25–35°C
- Soil: Sandy loam, pH 6.0–7.5
- Varieties: Pusa Vishal, SML-668, MH-421
- Seed rate: 20–25 kg/ha
- Duration: 60–75 days
- Yield: 1.0–1.5 t/ha

6. COMMON PRACTICES
- Seed treatment: Rhizobium culture + Thiram
- Fertilizer: 20 kg N + 60 kg P2O5/ha (basal)
- Weed management: Pendimethalin pre-emergence
- Harvesting: When 75–80% pods mature

7. NITROGEN FIXATION
- Pulses fix 50–200 kg N/ha through Rhizobium symbiosis
- Improve soil fertility for subsequent crops
- Intercropping with cereals improves system productivity
