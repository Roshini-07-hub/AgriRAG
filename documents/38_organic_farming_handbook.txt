ORGANIC FARMING HANDBOOK
========================

1. INTRODUCTION
Organic farming is a production system that sustains the health of soils, ecosystems, and people. It relies on ecological processes, biodiversity, and cycles adapted to local conditions, rather than synthetic inputs. India has the largest number of organic farmers globally (3.5 million).

2. PRINCIPLES OF ORGANIC FARMING
- Health: Maintain and enhance soil, plant, animal, and human health
- Ecology: Work with living ecological systems and cycles
- Fairness: Build relationships that ensure fairness for all
- Care: Manage in a precautionary and responsible manner

3. ORGANIC INPUTS

3.1 Organic Manures
- Farmyard Manure (FYM): 10–15 t/ha; NPK ratio 0.5:0.25:0.5%
- Compost: 5–10 t/ha; NPK ratio 1.5:1.0:1.5%
- Vermicompost: 2–5 t/ha; NPK ratio 2.5:1.5:1.5%
- Green manure: Dhaincha, Sunhemp, Sesbania (100–150 kg N/ha)
- Poultry manure: 5 t/ha; high N content

3.2 Biofertilizers
- Rhizobium: For legumes (fixes 50–200 kg N/ha)
- Azotobacter: For non-legumes (fixes 20–30 kg N/ha)
- Azospirillum: For cereals (fixes 20–40 kg N/ha)
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria): Solubilizes fixed P
- VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhiza): Improves P uptake

3.3 Organic Pest Management
- Neem-based products: NSKE 5%, Neem oil 3%
- Botanical pesticides: Pyrethrum, Rotenone
- Pheromone traps for monitoring
- Biological control agents

4. ORGANIC CERTIFICATION
- National Programme for Organic Production (NPOP)
- Participatory Guarantee System (PGS)
- Third-party certification by accredited agencies
- Conversion period: 2–3 years from conventional to organic

5. CROP ROTATION IN ORGANIC FARMING
- Legume-cereal rotation to maintain soil fertility
- Include green manure crops
- Avoid monoculture
- Example: Rice → Wheat → Legume → Vegetable

6. ORGANIC STANDARDS
- No synthetic fertilizers or pesticides
- No GMO seeds
- No sewage sludge
- No irradiation
- Maintain buffer zones from conventional fields

7. ECONOMICS OF ORGANIC FARMING
- Higher input costs initially
- Premium price: 20–50% above conventional
- Lower yield in transition period (10–20% reduction)
- Long-term soil health improvement
- Export potential: India exports organic products worth $1 billion+

8. CHALLENGES
- Lower yields in initial years
- Pest and disease management
- Certification costs
- Market access and price realization
- Knowledge and skill requirements
