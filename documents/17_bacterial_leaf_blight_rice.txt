BACTERIAL LEAF BLIGHT IN RICE
==============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB) of rice is caused by Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae. It is one of the most serious bacterial diseases of rice, causing yield losses of 20–30% in susceptible varieties.

2. SYMPTOMS
- Water-soaked to yellowish stripes on leaf margins
- Lesions turn straw-yellow and dry
- Kresek phase: Wilting and death of seedlings within 3 weeks of transplanting
- Yellow leaf phase: Yellowing of upper leaves in older plants
- Pale yellow phase: Yellowing of entire plant

3. FAVORABLE CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 25–34°C
- High humidity and rainfall
- Flood irrigation and strong winds
- Excess nitrogen application
- Injuries from insects or mechanical damage

4. DISEASE CYCLE
- Bacteria survive in infected plant debris and soil
- Enter through hydathodes (water pores) and wounds
- Spread through irrigation water, rain, and wind
- Systemic infection through vascular system

5. MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

5.1 Cultural Control
- Use resistant varieties: IR-20, Pankaj, Jaya, TN-1
- Avoid excess nitrogen (especially late application)
- Drain fields during kresek phase
- Remove and destroy infected plants
- Avoid flood irrigation during disease outbreak

5.2 Chemical Control
- Seed treatment: Streptomycin sulphate 0.025% + Copper oxychloride 0.3%
- Foliar spray:
  * Copper oxychloride 0.3% (3 g/L)
  * Streptomycin sulphate 200 ppm + Copper oxychloride 0.3%
  * Bactericide Kocide 2000 at 2 g/L
- Spray at first symptom appearance and repeat after 10 days

5.3 Biological Control
- Pseudomonas fluorescens 0.5% WP at 2.5 kg/ha
- Bacillus subtilis seed treatment

6. INTEGRATED MANAGEMENT
- Combine resistant varieties + balanced nutrition + timely chemical spray
- Avoid transplanting from infected nurseries
- Maintain field sanitation

7. ECONOMIC IMPORTANCE
- Kresek phase can cause 100% loss in nursery
- Leaf blight phase causes 20–30% yield loss
- Quality of grain is also affected
