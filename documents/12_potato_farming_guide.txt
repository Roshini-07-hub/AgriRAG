POTATO FARMING GUIDE
====================

1. INTRODUCTION
Potato (Solanum tuberosum) is the world's fourth largest food crop. It is rich in carbohydrates, vitamin C, and potassium. In India, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Bihar, and Punjab are major producers.

2. CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 15–20°C for tuber formation
- Cool nights (10–15°C) promote tuber development
- Frost damages foliage but not tubers if covered
- Requires 90–120 frost-free days

3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils
- pH: 5.5–6.5
- Deep, loose soils for tuber expansion
- Avoid heavy clay and waterlogged soils

4. VARIETIES
- Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Chandramukhi (early)
- Kufri Sindhuri, Kufri Badshah (mid-season)
- Kufri Pukhraj, Kufri Chipsona (processing)
- Kufri Himalini (hills)

5. LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing to 30–35 cm
- 2–3 harrowings for fine tilth
- Apply 25 t/ha FYM before planting
- Form ridges at 60 cm spacing

6. PLANTING
- Rabi season: October–November
- Seed rate: 2.0–2.5 t/ha (whole tubers or cut pieces)
- Spacing: 60×20 cm
- Depth: 8–10 cm
- Treat seed tubers with Mancozeb 0.25% solution

7. EARTHING UP
- First earthing up at 30 DAS
- Second earthing up at 45–50 DAS
- Prevents greening of tubers
- Promotes tuber development

8. IRRIGATION
- First irrigation immediately after planting
- Irrigate every 7–10 days
- Stop irrigation 10–15 days before harvest
- Total requirement: 350–500 mm

9. FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Nitrogen: 150–180 kg/ha (2 splits)
- Phosphorus: 100 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium: 120 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium improves tuber quality and storage

10. HARVESTING
- Harvest when foliage turns yellow and dies
- Maturity: 70–120 days depending on variety
- Dry tubers in shade for 2–3 days before storage
- Average yield: 20–30 t/ha

11. STORAGE
- Store at 2–4°C for long-term storage
- Maintain 90–95% relative humidity
- Cold storage capacity: 10–15 tonnes/room
