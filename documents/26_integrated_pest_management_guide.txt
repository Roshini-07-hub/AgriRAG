INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) GUIDE
=======================================

1. INTRODUCTION
Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is an ecosystem-based strategy that focuses on long-term prevention of pests through a combination of techniques. IPM minimizes risks to human health, beneficial organisms, and the environment while maintaining economic viability.

2. PRINCIPLES OF IPM
- Prevention: Avoid pest problems through cultural practices
- Monitoring: Regular scouting to assess pest populations
- Threshold: Act only when pest populations exceed economic threshold
- Control: Use least-toxic methods first
- Evaluation: Assess effectiveness of control measures

3. COMPONENTS OF IPM

3.1 Cultural Control
- Crop rotation to break pest cycles
- Resistant varieties
- Proper planting time and spacing
- Sanitation: Remove crop debris
- Trap crops to attract pests away from main crop
- Intercropping to disrupt pest habitat

3.2 Mechanical and Physical Control
- Hand picking of insects and egg masses
- Light traps for nocturnal insects
- Yellow sticky traps for whiteflies and aphids
- Pheromone traps for monitoring and mass trapping
- Barriers and nets to exclude pests
- Soil solarization for soil-borne pests

3.3 Biological Control
- Predators: Chrysoperla carnea, Coccinella septempunctata
- Parasitoids: Trichogramma spp., Bracon spp.
- Pathogens: Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), NPV, Beauveria bassiana
- Nematodes: Steinernema, Heterorhabditis
- Conservation of natural enemies

3.4 Chemical Control (Last Resort)
- Use selective, less toxic pesticides
- Apply at economic threshold level
- Rotate pesticides to prevent resistance
- Follow label instructions and safety precautions
- Avoid broad-spectrum pesticides during flowering

4. ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVELS (ETL)
- Rice stem borer: 5% dead hearts or 10% white ears
- Cotton bollworm: 5 larvae per 10 plants
- Aphids: 10–15 per leaf
- Whitefly: 6 per leaf

5. MONITORING TOOLS
- Pheromone traps: 5 per hectare
- Yellow sticky traps: 10–15 per hectare
- Light traps: 1 per 5 hectares
- Regular field scouting: Weekly

6. BENEFITS OF IPM
- Reduces pesticide use by 30–50%
- Preserves natural enemies
- Reduces production costs
- Improves food safety
- Protects environment and human health
