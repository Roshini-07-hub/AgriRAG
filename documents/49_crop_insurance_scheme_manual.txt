CROP INSURANCE SCHEME MANUAL
=============================

1. INTRODUCTION
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) was launched on 18 February 2016. It provides comprehensive insurance coverage against crop failure due to non-preventable natural risks, stabilizing farmers' income and encouraging modern agricultural practices.

2. SCHEME OBJECTIVES
- Provide financial support to farmers suffering crop loss
- Stabilize farm income to ensure continuity in farming
- Encourage adoption of innovative and modern agricultural practices
- Ensure flow of credit to agriculture sector

3. COVERAGE

3.1 Crops Covered
- Food crops: Cereals, millets, pulses
- Oilseeds
- Annual commercial/horticultural crops
- Perennial horticultural crops (pilot basis)

3.2 Risks Covered
- Natural fire and lightning
- Storm, hailstorm, cyclone, typhoon, tempest, hurricane, tornado
- Flood, inundation, and landslide
- Drought, dry spells
- Pests and diseases
- Post-harvest losses (up to 14 days for specific crops)
- Localized calamities: Hailstorm, landslide, inundation

4. PREMIUM RATES
- Kharif crops: 2% of sum insured (farmer's share)
- Rabi crops: 1.5% of sum insured
- Annual commercial/horticultural crops: 5%
- Remaining premium paid by Central and State governments

5. SUM INSURED
- Based on Scale of Finance (SOF) fixed by District Level Technical Committee
- Covers cost of cultivation
- Minimum sum insured = Scale of Finance

6. ELIGIBILITY
- All farmers growing notified crops in notified areas
- Loanee farmers: Compulsory coverage
- Non-loanee farmers: Voluntary

7. ENROLLMENT PROCESS
- Loanee farmers: Through banks at time of crop loan
- Non-loanee farmers: Through banks, CSCs, insurance companies
- Online enrollment at pmfby.gov.in
- Documents: Aadhaar, bank account, land records, sowing certificate

8. CLAIM PROCESS
- Crop loss assessment by government officials
- Yield-based claims: Comparison with threshold yield
- Individual farm-level assessment for localized calamities
- Claim settlement within 2 months of harvest

9. TECHNOLOGY USE
- Remote sensing for crop cutting experiments
- Smartphone app for loss reporting
- Drone technology for damage assessment
- Weather stations for weather-based index insurance

10. RESTRUCTURED PMFBY (2020)
- Voluntary for all farmers (not compulsory for loanee farmers)
- State governments can opt in/out
- Increased use of technology
- Faster claim settlement

11. WEATHER BASED CROP INSURANCE (WBCIS)
- Based on weather parameters (rainfall, temperature, humidity)
- Faster claim settlement
- No crop cutting experiments needed
- Suitable for horticulture crops
